    • Monday, May 28, 1979; around the time everyone was at the Memorial Day parade I was being brought into this world in the small city of Merced, California.

    Growing up I was an eager child helping my only true father my stepdad Mark in the garage repairing cars, and whatever new and interesting mechanical devices that need to be repaired. My mother was going to school at the time for art. When she came home she would teach my older sister Mary, and I about art. Art has always fascinated me, the way an artist can recreate the world around them with the stroke of a brush.

    When I was three years old we moved from Merced to North Highlands, California and as the time past by I kept studying art, music, and mechanics. But I grew more interested in other things like the Military. So when I turned eighteen years old after graduating from Highlands High School I signed up for the United States Marine Corps as an AV8B Harrier Jet engine mechanic. I served five and a half years in the support of Operation Enduring Freedom. As my service requirement was met I was honorably discharged.

    Then I followed my family to Pennsylvania where I got a job in electronics repairing radios. Working in the day and going to school at night I was quickly getting ran down. So I switch my work shift to nights and received an Associate in Computer Networking Systems from ITT-Technical Inst. Dunmore Campus. It was that decision to switch to nights that led us to the idea that set us on this great adventure that would be Solar Storm Studios.

    Mathaniel Avery
    Solar Storm Studios
